2. Philosophical Foundations

At the heart of Dr. Ann’s model lie four interlocking philosophical pillars that challenge conventional health paradigms:

2.1. ICF: From Disease to Daily Performance

Traditional biomedical models prioritize disease classification and intervention. In contrast, the ICF framework shifts focus to functioning: how individuals perform activities (e.g., walking, speaking) and participate in life roles (e.g., working, socializing). Functioning is contextual. Dr. Ann reframes health not as a diagnostic label but as a continuum of capabilities.

2.2. Human-AI Collaboration: Conversations over Catalogs

Dr. Ann replaces "distant data" with conversational context. Generative AI agents (ChatGPT, Claude, LINER) engage individuals in dialogue, fostering real-time, personalized insights. AI becomes a companion in health co-creation.

Imagine an AI Health Companion: What kind of questions would you want it to ask you about your daily well-being?

2.3. Self-Management: Ownership of Health

Individuals are empowered as sovereign agents: they choose routines, respond to AI feedback, and adjust goals. Health becomes a practice of daily self-measurement and reflection.

2.4. Local Storage Sovereignty: Data Autonomy

Individuals retain control over their functioning data, stored on personal devices or secure local platforms. This respects autonomy and protects sensitive information.

Together, these pillars establish a reorientation: from institutional authority to individual agency, static measures to adaptive capacities, centralized control to distributed autonomy, and expert dictums to conversational co-creation.

3. Assetization of Health

Dr. Ann’s insight: health is a transformable asset—measured, narrated, improved, and circulated.

3.1. Measurement via Rasch-Scaled Logit MappingA psychometric method to convert ordinal responses into interval measures.

Rasch analysis converts ordinal responses (e.g., "I can walk for 10 minutes") into interval-level measures on a logit scale. Participants engage in adaptive assessments to calibrate functioning thresholds, resulting in a personal logit map.

Try a simplified self-assessment (conceptual): On a scale of 1 (Very Difficult) to 5 (Very Easy), how easy is it for you to walk for 30 minutes without stopping?

A real Rasch assessment is more complex and adaptive.

3.2. Narration via HandLoop™

HandLoop™ is an AI-assisted journaling system. Individuals narrate daily routines, emotional states, and contextual variables. AI guides users with structured prompts. These narratives are time-stamped, categorized, and linked to logit scores.

HandLoop™ Prompt Example: "Describe how you started your morning routine today. What went well? What was challenging?"

3.3. Transformation through AI-Human Loops

AI algorithms analyze journal entries and logit data to identify patterns and propose personalized interventions. Users review, retain, and implement modified routines. This cycle fosters behavioral resilience.

3.4. Trading via Community Loops

Health routines and insights become community assets. In "health markets," individuals share, donate, or trade functioning assets (e.g., mobility exercises, coping strategies). Health circulates as a shared social good.

Assetizing health aligns public health with economics and sustainability: measurement enables valuation, narration creates narratives for investment, transformation drives return on well-being, and trading fosters community engagement.

4. MyLifeIndex: A Living Ecosystem

MyLifeIndex is a digital ecosystem operationalizing functioning-based public health.

4.1. Data Collection: Multidimensional Functioning Inputs

  • Wearable Sensors: Track steps, posture, physiological metrics.
  • Self-Assessment Prompts: Via AI chat for mood, cognitive clarity, pain.
  • Social Participation Logs: Record interactions.
  • Environmental Scans: Geolocation to contextualize functioning.

All inputs feed into a unified data warehouse managed locally on the user’s device.
